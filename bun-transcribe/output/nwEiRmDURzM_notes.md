## Main Events (2 Kings 6:8-23)
- Syrian king plots against Israel; Elisha warns King Jehoram, foiling plans repeatedly.
- Syria learns of Elisha; surrounds Dothan (cf. Genesis 37:17, Joseph's pit).
- Servant panics; Elisha: "Do not fear, those with us > those with them."
- Elisha prays: servant sees mountain full of fiery horses/chariots protecting them.
- Elisha prays: Syrian army blinded; leads them 12 miles to Samaria.
- Elisha: Feed them; great feast given; raiders cease invading Israel.

## Siege of Samaria (2 Kings 6:24-7:1)
- Ben-Hadad besieges; extreme famine (donkey head $500; dove droppings $30).
- Cannibalism: Women eat son.
- King blames Elisha, vows his death; Elisha anticipates, instructs elders.
- King: "Why wait on Lord?"
- Elisha prophesies abundance tomorrow; doubting officer: "Even if windows opened in heaven?" Elisha: "You will see but not eat."

## Lepers' Discovery (2 Kings 7:3-9)
- 4 lepers surrender to Syrian camp; find it abandoned.
- God causes Syrians to hear phantom army noise (Hittites/Egyptians); they flee.
- Lepers loot, then feel guilty: "This is good news; we must share."

## Key Principles
- God softens hard hearts (axe head floats).
- God present/working when unseen (open spiritual eyes).
- Let enemies see Lord through grace/mercy (feed enemies).
- Share good news; don't stay silent (gospel application).
